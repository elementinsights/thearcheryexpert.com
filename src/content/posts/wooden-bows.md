faq:
  - question: "What type of wood is best for a traditional wooden bow?"
    answer: "Yew, hickory, and bamboo are among the most popular choices for wooden bows. Yew has been used for centuries in English longbows because of its natural combination of compression and tension strength. Laminated bows often combine multiple wood types for improved performance and durability."
  - question: "How do I care for a wooden bow to make it last?"
    answer: "Always unstring your wooden bow when it's not in use to prevent the limbs from developing a permanent set. Store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. Applying a light coat of bowstring wax and periodically treating the wood with a quality finish will keep it in top condition."
  - question: "Are wooden bows good for hunting or just target shooting?"
    answer: "Wooden bows work well for both hunting and target shooting, though they require more skill than modern compound bows. Many traditional bowhunters prefer wooden longbows and recurves for the challenge and quiet shooting they provide. Just make sure your draw weight is high enough for ethical hunting, usually 40 pounds or more for deer-sized game."
