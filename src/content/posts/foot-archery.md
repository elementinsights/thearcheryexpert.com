## What's Foot Archery?

As explained earlier, foot archery, or more popularly known as Contortion Archery, is a form of archery that uses the leg and toes to draw and aim at your targets.

Such discipline is in use for centuries now, but it's not typically used in war during those early periods mainly because it's impractical.

They may also be very ineffective since aiming at a moving target is almost impossible.

The mechanism to draw in a traditional bow is straightforward.

However, it's going to be a lot harder to pull it off if you're not drawing it from your arms.

And to add the level of difficulty, the performer's position is often awkward and contorted manner - thus, it's where the name contortion artist came from.

The back of the archer is bent, with their hips over their heads.

As you see, even getting in this position already possesses a lot of challenges.

Gaining power and maintaining momentum requires power from your legs. And the ability to draw and hold your aim requires a strong grip from your toes.

Overall, a strong upper body and balance are the key for you to succeed in this form of discipline.

And therefore, strength training that especially focuses on the upper torso is extremely beneficial because it'll hold your balance.

I'll explain this in the later section of this content.

I also mentioned in the introduction that there are no governing or specific bodies that regulate this form of archery.

For one good reason, it's generally not a sport recognized by any organization.

Also, it's not a competition-worthy act, in my opinion.

Like mounted archery, foot archery isn't part of the Olympics.

And you may only see them in a circus act.
