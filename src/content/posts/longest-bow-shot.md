## Farthest Archery Target Hit (Male)

On December 9, 2015, Matt Stutzman successfully hit a target with a distance of 930 ft (283.4 meters).

This was the farthest accurate shot in modern archery under World Archery Conditions.

Matt Stutzman was a disabled man and a member of the U.S. Paralympic Archery Team in 2012.

The shot was completed under FITA (Fédération Internationale de Tir à l'Arc) conditions.

An able-bodied person previously held the record.

## Farthest Shot in Flight Archery

In 1987 Don Brown shot an arrow at an amazing distance of 1,336 yards 1’3” (1,222 meters).

Don Brown accomplished the record with a handheld Unlimited Conventional Flight Bow.

For Unlimited LongBow, Jeremy Spencer holds the best record with 415 yards (379.51 meters).

The Great Harry Drake was able to do farther, with a still unbeaten record of 2,047 yards (1871 meters).

Harry Drake did this in 1988 but with a Flight Crossbow or Unlimited Foot bow.

Harry Drake was a pioneer bowyer and made significant contributions in designing and building the early modern “Composite Bows.”

He also built the first-ever bow to shoot more than 600 yards since the day of Ancient Turks.

Recorded in the Southern California Archery Association Tournament on May 31, 1947.

A Drake bow designed in 1964 was able to shoot and record a distance of 1,077 yards on October 24, 1971, in the Official North America Archery Flight Championships.

[Drake Bows are very popular](http://www.vintagearchery.org/drake.html) and has a huge reputation for being very powerful, accurate, smooth, excellent shooters, and a tendency to not stack.

## Fastest Three-Circuit Underwater Archery Course Completion

Yohann Belpre (France) achieved the fastest three-circuit underwater archery course in just 54.74 seconds on the set of 'Officially Amazing' (CBBC) in Lyon, France, on 24 June 2014.

## Greatest Height to Shoot and Catch an Arrow

Anthony Kelly (Australia) achieved the greatest height to shoot and then catch an arrow at 51.2 m (167.979 ft.), in New South Wales, Australia, on 12 December 2018.

## Heaviest Longbow Draw Weight

The maximum draw on an arrow was achieved by Mark Stretton (UK) after drawing a longbow weighing 90 kg (200 lb.) to the length 82.5 cm (32 ½ in).

The bow was made of yew wood-backed with hickory by Bickerstaff Bows (UK).

The arrow was made of aspen (poplar) and fired at a target approximately 5 yards away from the archer.

It was recorded on 15 August 2004 at the shooting grounds of The Bath Archers, Somerset, UK.
