faq:
  - question: "What's the difference between arrow vanes and feather fletching?"
    answer: "Vanes are made from synthetic materials like plastic or rubber, while feather fletchings come from real bird feathers. Vanes are more durable and weather-resistant, making them popular for hunting. Feathers are lighter and more forgiving for finger shooters, but they can get damaged by rain."
  - question: "What size arrow vanes should I use?"
    answer: "For hunting with broadheads, larger vanes (2 to 4 inches) help stabilize the arrow faster after release. For target shooting with field points, smaller vanes (1.5 to 2 inches) reduce drag and work well at longer distances. Your arrow's spine and point weight also factor into the ideal vane size."
  - question: "How often should I replace my arrow vanes?"
    answer: "Replace vanes when you notice visible damage like tears, curling, or separation from the shaft. With regular shooting, you might need to replace them every few months. Inspect your vanes before each session, and don't shoot with damaged fletchings because it'll hurt your accuracy."

Remember that scene in old war movies, where the protagonist releases an arrow into the air and watches it soar effortlessly across the battlefield to meet the intended target?

While it may have looked like all it took was sheer strength and impeccable aim, it really was the arrow vane than anything else.

Ask any archer or hunter, and they'll tell you this -  arrow vanes are just as, if not more, important as the bow and arrow.

They really should be more talked about, but the term 'bow and arrow' is set in stone, and there's nothing we can do about it.

Arrow vanes determine the stability and aim of the arrow during its flight.

The better the quality of the vane, the better the stability and aim will be.
